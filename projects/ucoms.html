Ubiquitous Computing and Monitoring System (UCoMS) for Discovery and Management of Energy Resources

Nian-Feng Tzeng
Center for Advanced Computer Studies
University of Louisiana at Lafayette




This UCoMS (Ubiquitous Computing and Monitoring System) project was a major, collaborative research initiative funded by the U.S. Department of Energy (DoE) and the Louisiana Board of Regents. It brought together researchers from UL Lafayette, LSU, and Southern University to conduct research topics centering on a computing paradigm where processing cabilities and sensing technologies are invisibly embedded into everyday objects and environments for boosting Gulf petroleum productivity and energy-resource management via "ubiquitous computing."

The project built a unified grid portal and a "Black Oil Toolkit," aiming to integrated large-scale scientific modeling, grid computing and real-time data collection from wireless sensor networked deployed in the field.
