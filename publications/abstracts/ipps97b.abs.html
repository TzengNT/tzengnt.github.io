Barrier Synchronization and Data Movement Reduction in Distributed Shared Memory Systems

Abstract -- This paper introduces an effective approach for barrier synchronization in the distributed shared memory (DSM) systems under release consistency. The proposed approach is based on a binomial spanning tree and is aimed to reduce network contention and to maximize parallelism in the barrier release process. It gives rise to reduction in the total number of messages transmitted over the network, because reduced contention and faster barrier release lower the number of retransmitted messages. This reduction translates to performance improvement, and the degree of improvement increases quickly as the number of participants grows. A technique for reducing the data movement amount is also presented to eliminate many cold misses in TreadMarks, the best know software-based DSM. We incorporated the proposed barrier approach and the data movement reduction technique in TreadMarks for evaluation using four benchmark programs on a network of workstations. The IBM SP machine at Argonne National Laboratory is also employed to evaluate the proposed barrier approach when more participants are involved in the barrier. Experimental results are gathered and demonstrated.

Index Terms -- barrier synchronization, cold misses, distributed shared memory, memory consistency, message transmission, network contention, networks of workstations, spanning trees.
